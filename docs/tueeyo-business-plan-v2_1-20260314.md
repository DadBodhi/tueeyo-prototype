## Executive Summary

### The Opportunity

The dance community in the UK and beyond faces a critical infrastructure crisis. Facebook, once the primary platform for discovering classes, coordinating events, and maintaining community connections, has degraded to the point where it actively harms rather than helps. Generic event platforms like Eventbrite and Meetup charge high fees (8%+) while lacking dance-specific features and understanding of the unique needs of partner dance communities.

**Tueeyo** addresses this gap with a purpose-built platform for dancers that combines event discovery, professional web presence for teachers/DJs/photographers, integrated payment handling, and community features. We're building what Facebook used to be, enhanced with features that generic platforms cannot provide.

### The Solution

**For Students/Dancers:**
- Discover classes by style, level, location, teacher (better than Facebook)
- Check-in to build attendance history and earn achievements
- Access class routine videos (teachers upload, students access by checking in)
- Pay for classes through unified wallet with bonus credits
- Connect with dance partners and community

**For Professionals (Teachers, DJs, Photographers, Promoters):**

**Free Tier (£0/month):**
- Claim up to 6 events per month
- Full features on 1 "featured" activity (video uploads, messaging, updates)
- Basic features on other activities (tracking, listings)
- Test platform, build presence, no commitment

**Plus Tier (£20/month + 3% transaction fee):**
- **Professional website:** yourname.tueeyo.uk (custom subdomain)
- Fully customizable landing page (branding, bio, gallery, reviews)
- **Payment processing:** Accept bookings, sell packages/memberships
- Full features on ALL activities (unlimited videos, messaging, updates)
- Advanced analytics (retention, revenue, trends)
- Waitlist management, promotional tools

**Pro Tier (£50/month + 3% transaction fee):**
- Everything in Plus
- Organization subdomain: schoolname.tueeyo.uk
- Multi-user management (teachers, staff, permissions)
- Consolidated dashboard and analytics
- School-wide payment and reporting

### Business Model

**Dual Revenue Streams:**

1. **Subscription Revenue (Primary):**
   - Plus: £20/month (professionals)
   - Pro: £50/month (organizations)
   - Target: 10-15% conversion from Free tier

2. **Transaction Fees (Secondary):**
   - 3% of payments processed through platform
   - Gradually increasing to 5% over 24 months
   - Lower than Eventbrite (8%+), competitive with Stripe direct (2.9%)

**Market Size (UK):**
- ~300 weekly recurring classes across major cities
- ~9,000 regular dancers (attending 2+ times per month)
- Realistic penetration: 60% of market within 5 years
- Addressable market: £30-40k annual revenue at maturity

**This is a lifestyle business, not a VC-scale opportunity.** Profitable, sustainable, valuable to community, but regional/niche in scope.

## Market Analysis

### Market Size & Segmentation

#### A Two-Axis Growth Model

Tueeyo's addressable market expands along two independent axes:

- **Geographic:** Leeds → UK cities → International (Ireland, Spain, US Latin scenes)
- **Dance style:** Latin/Salsa → Partner dance broadly → All class-based dance styles (Phase 3)

These axes are independent. Geographic expansion can happen within Latin dance before style expansion begins, and vice versa. This creates natural decision points and multiple paths to growth without requiring a single large market from day one.

#### UK Market — Tiered Breakdown

| Tier | Community | Est. UK regulars | Tueeyo fit | Launch timing |
|------|-----------|-----------------|------------|---------------|
| **Beachhead** | Latin dance (salsa, bachata, kizomba) | ~18,000 | Perfect — built for this | Year 1–2 |
| **Adjacent** | Other partner dance (tango, ceroc, swing) | ~40,000 | Strong — same platform needs | Year 2–3 |
| **Broader** | All class-based dance styles | 100,000+ | Good — minor adaptation | Year 4+ |

**Total realistic addressable market (Tiers 1–2): ~55,000–60,000 regular UK class attendees.**

**Basis for estimates:**
The Leeds salsa Facebook group has 5,200 members with ~200 daily active viewers, suggesting 400–600 genuinely active Leeds Latin dancers. Leeds is a significant but mid-sized UK scene. Scaled nationally across ~30 cities, 15,000–20,000 regular Latin dancers is a conservative estimate. The ceroc/Modern Jive community alone operates 200+ UK venues with typical attendance of 80–150 per venue.

**Style overlap — important for launch modelling:**
Dancers are not siloed. Within the Latin community there is approximately 70% overlap between salsa, bachata, and kizomba; ~20% bleed into Argentine tango; and 10–15% into ceroc/Modern Jive. Activating the Leeds salsa scene therefore captures a significant proportion of the Leeds Latin community as a whole without separate targeting.

#### The Community Flywheel

Tueeyo's growth follows a **scene-based flywheel** driven by the highly connected nature of dance communities. A small group of "super node" regulars attend multiple classes per week across schools. They activate the platform quickly in each new scene:

1. Super nodes check in → platform looks active to teachers immediately
2. Teachers claim events → can message their students for the first time
3. Students check in more consistently → teacher gains reputation from each check-in
4. Teacher uploads routine video → gated content drives further student sign-ups
5. Teacher upgrades to Plus → wallet and payments spread across schools
6. Scene reaches critical mass → next city or style activates faster

**Each new city or dance style restarts this flywheel.** The scene — not the individual user — is the primary unit of growth.

#### Major Events as Acquisition Accelerators

Dance communities gather at large regional and national events (e.g. Calle Ocho Leeds: ~200–300 attendees; Blackpool salsa nights: ~700 attendees). These events draw dancers from multiple cities in a single night, and occur several times per year.

A single major event can onboard 50–150 new users from 5–10 different cities, activating super nodes in scenes where Tueeyo had no prior presence. These are recurring acquisition opportunities, not one-off windfalls.

#### Target Market (Year 1–2)

- **Year 1:** Leeds Latin dance community (~400–600 active regulars across salsa, bachata, kizomba)
- **Year 2:** Northern England Latin + first adjacent scenes activated via major events

#### Customer Segments

**Primary Segments:**

**1. Students/Dancers (90% of users, £0 revenue directly)**
- Age: 25-55 (peak 30-45)
- Income: £25k-60k (middle class)
- Motivation: Fitness, social, hobby, partner activity
- Pain Points:
  - Finding classes (Facebook groups unreliable)
  - Discovering new styles/teachers
  - Tracking progress
  - Connecting with community post-COVID
- Value Proposition:
  - Comprehensive event discovery
  - Attendance tracking & gamification
  - Access to class videos (gated by check-in)
  - Social connections ("connected with")
  - Unified wallet (convenience, pre-load discount)

**2. Teachers (7% of users, primary revenue source)**

**Core insight:** Teacher is not a separate user type — it is an advanced form of dancer. Many teachers started as students on the platform, and their dance history and community reputation are visible before they teach their first class. The journey from dancer to teacher is a natural progression, not a separate onboarding flow.

**The primary teacher pain point is not videos — it is communication.** Teachers currently have no reliable way to reach their own students. No email list, no phone numbers, no WhatsApp group. Students show up, pay cash, and leave. A cancelled class means posting on Facebook and hoping the algorithm shows it. Tueeyo solves this automatically: every student who checks in has implicitly opted into communications from that teacher. The contact list builds itself.

- **Hobbyists (Free Tier):**
  - Teaching 1 class per week
  - £200-400/month income from teaching
  - Part-time, side hustle
  - Pain: No way to reach students, administrative overhead
  - Value: Claim event → instant group messaging to attendees → cancel/update classes reliably

- **Professionals (Plus Tier Target):**
  - Teaching 2-5 classes per week
  - £800-2,000/month income from teaching
  - Dance is primary or significant income
  - Pain: Professional image, payment chasing, retention insights
  - Value: Custom subdomain (worth £500-2,000 to build yourself), payment automation, analytics

- **Schools (Pro Tier Target):**
  - 3+ teachers, 10+ classes per week
  - £5,000-15,000/month revenue
  - Established business
  - Pain: Multi-teacher coordination, payment splits, student flow
  - Value: Consolidated management, organisation branding, analytics

**3. DJs (2% of users)**
- Booking frequency: 2-8 gigs per month
- Fee per gig: £100-300
- Pain: Booking management, portfolio showcase, getting discovered
- Value: Professional page (djname.tueeyo.uk), booking system, integrated into dance community

**4. Photographers (<1% of users)**
- Sessions per month: 2-10
- Fee per session: £50-150
- Pain: Getting discovered, booking management, portfolio
- Value: Professional page, booking system, integrated into events

**5. Event Promoters/Organizers (1% of users)**
- Events per month: 1-4
- Revenue per event: £200-1,000 (ticket sales)
- Pain: Event promotion, ticket sales (Eventbrite fees 8%+)
- Value: Cheaper fees (3%), integrated into dance community, attendee management

### Competitive Landscape

#### Direct Competitors

**1. Facebook Events/Groups**
- **Market Share:** 70%+ of event promotion
- **Strengths:**
  - Ubiquitous (everyone has account)
  - Free for everyone
  - Network effects (friends already there)
  - Familiarity
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Algorithm-driven (unreliable reach)
  - Poor search/discovery
  - Event spam
  - No dance-specific features
  - No payment integration
  - Declining trust/usage
- **Our Strategy:**
  - Position as "what Facebook used to be" + dance-specific upgrades
  - Chronological, reliable, no algorithm manipulation
  - Feature parity on basics, exceed on dance features
  - Not trying to replace Facebook for all social, just dance organization

**2. Eventbrite**
- **Market Share:** 15-20% of paid dance events
- **Pricing:** 2-5% + £0.59-0.99 per ticket (effectively 8-10% for £8 class)
- **Strengths:**
  - Established, trusted brand
  - Professional ticketing system
  - Good reporting
  - Mainstream recognition
- **Weaknesses:**
  - High fees (8-10% vs our 3-5%)
  - Not designed for recurring classes
  - No community features
  - Generic (not dance-specific)
  - No social layer
- **Our Strategy:**
  - Undercut on price significantly
  - Better for recurring classes (our core use case)
  - Social + community layer they don't have
  - Dance-specific features (styles, levels, videos)

**3. Meetup**
- **Market Share:** 5-10% of dance groups
- **Pricing:** $15-45/month for organizers
- **Strengths:**
  - Established community platform
  - Group management features
  - Discovery for new users
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Flat fee (not revenue share)
  - No payment integration
  - Generic platform
  - Declining relevance
- **Our Strategy:**
  - Free tier vs their flat fee (lower barrier)
  - Integrated payments (they don't have)
  - Revenue share vs flat cost (aligns incentives)
  - Dance-specific

**4. DanceBug / Similar Dance Directories**
- **Market Share:** <5%, declining
- **Strengths:**
  - Dance-specific
  - Established SEO
  - Comprehensive listings (historically)
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Passive directory (no engagement features)
  - Outdated UI/UX
  - No social features
  - No payment integration
  - Declining usage
- **Our Strategy:**
  - Full platform vs passive directory
  - Modern UX/UI
  - Social + payment features
  - Mobile-first (they're desktop legacy)

#### Indirect Competitors

**WhatsApp/Telegram Groups:**
- Usage: 60%+ for class communication
- Relationship: Complementary, not competitive
- Teachers use WhatsApp for chat, Tueeyo for structure
- We drive traffic to WhatsApp (and vice versa)

**Instagram:**
- Usage: Promotional, discovery
- Relationship: Complementary
- Teachers use Instagram for marketing
- Tueeyo page in Instagram bio drives bookings
- Instagram brings traffic to Tueeyo

**Direct Booking (Teacher Websites):**
- Small percentage have own websites
- Usually static, outdated, no payment integration
- Tueeyo custom subdomain is easier + better

### Market Trends

**Favorable:**

1. **Facebook Decline:**
   - Dancers actively seeking alternatives
   - Event reach declining (algorithm changes)
   - Groups cluttered, unreliable
   - Our research: 70% of Leeds dancers frustrated with Facebook

2. **Creator Economy:**
   - People expect professional tools (websites, payment handling)
   - Willing to pay for tools that help them earn
   - Substack, Patreon, Gumroad prove model
   - £20/month for professional page + payment = standard expectation

3. **Payment Expectations:**
   - Cashless society accelerating
   - Students expect digital payment options
   - Teachers tired of cash handling
   - Wallet systems normalized (Apple Pay, Venmo, Revolut)

4. **Post-COVID Rebuild:**
   - Dance community reorganizing
   - Need new infrastructure (old systems broken)
   - Opportune time for new platform
   - Teachers willing to try new tools

5. **Mobile-First:**
   - 80%+ access via smartphones
   - QR code check-ins feel natural
   - App-based solutions expected

**Unfavorable:**

1. **Economic Uncertainty:**
   - Dance classes discretionary spending
   - Recession could reduce attendance
   - However: Affordable (£8/class vs £40/month gym)
   - Social + exercise combo relatively resilient

2. **Market Size:**
   - Niche market (9,000 regular UK dancers)
   - Not VC-scale opportunity
   - Limits growth ceiling
   - But: Profitable at small scale possible

3. **Platform Fatigue:**
   - Users tired of new platforms
   - "Another thing to sign up for"
   - Mitigation: Immediate value (directory works without account)
   - Gradual engagement (check-in optional, then habitual)

### Competitive Advantages

Once established, Tueeyo creates defensibility through:

**1. Network Effects (Primary Moat):**
- More students → more valuable to teachers (attendance data, bookings)
- More teachers → more valuable to students (comprehensive listings, videos)
- More Plus tier users → more pressure on non-adopters (students expect wallet)
- Cross-school pollination (students attend multiple schools)
- Hard to leave once community is on platform

**2. Data Moat:**
- Attendance history (students can't take with them)
- Reviews and ratings (built up over time)
- Connections ("danced with" graph)
- Teacher analytics (retention, revenue insights)
- Switching costs: Lose all this data if leave platform

**3. Habit Formation:**
- Students check Tueeyo first for events
- Teachers upload videos here
- Routine becomes muscle memory
- Behavior change hard to reverse

**4. Integration Costs:**
- Plus tier teachers invested time customizing page
- Payment integration set up (Stripe connected)
- Student base already here (can't take to competitor)
- ROI already realized (sunk cost)

**5. Brand Association:**
- "The platform for dancers"
- First-mover in region
- Community-trusted (by dancers, for dancers)
- Generic platforms can't replicate this positioning

### Unit Economics

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):**

**Year 1-2 (Organic):**
- CAC ≈ £0 (organic growth, no paid marketing)
- Time investment (founder sweat equity)

**Year 3+ (With Paid Marketing):**
- Marketing spend: £6,000/year
- New Plus tier users: 10/year
- CAC (Plus tier): £600

**Lifetime Value (LTV):**

**Plus Tier User:**
- Monthly: £20 subscription + £15 transaction fees (avg) = £35
- Lifetime: 24 months (average - conservative)
- LTV: £35 × 24 = £840

**LTV:CAC Ratio:**
- £840 / £600 = 1.4:1
- Below ideal (3:1), but acceptable for early stage
- Improves with scale (CAC decreases, LTV increases with lower churn)

**Break-Even per Plus Tier User:**
- Monthly cost allocation: £50 (server, support, development amortized)
- Monthly revenue: £35
- **Not profitable per user at current scale**
- Requires: Higher pricing (£25+), lower costs, or longer lifetime

**Conclusion:** Current unit economics challenging. Requires:
- Lean operations (lower cost per user)
- Longer user lifetimes (reduce churn to <2%/month)
- Higher ARPU (packages, memberships increase transaction fees)
- Scale (fixed costs spread over more users)

## Risk Analysis & Mitigation

### Critical Risks

**1. Low Adoption (HIGH RISK)**

**Description:** Students don't create accounts, teachers don't engage

**Probability:** Medium (30-40%)

**Impact:** High (project fails)

**Mitigation:**
- Directory provides value without account (lower barrier)
- Gamification hooks students (badges, achievements)
- Teacher videos create pull demand (students want access)
- Free tier removes financial barrier
- Organic growth strategy (no forced adoption)
- 2-week prototype test validates appetite before full build

**Validation:**
- Phase 2 metrics (100+ students, 50+ check-ins/week)
- If metrics missed, pivot or stop before major investment

---

**2. Payment Adoption Failure (MEDIUM RISK)**

**Description:** Teachers don't upgrade to Plus, students don't use wallet

**Probability:** Medium (20-30%)

**Impact:** High (revenue model fails, but platform could survive as free community tool)

**Mitigation:**
- Plus tier valuable beyond payments (custom subdomain!)
- Free tier demonstrates value before paywall
- Low fees (3% vs 8%) very competitive
- Packages/memberships drive revenue per student
- Not dependent on 100% payment adoption
- Multi-revenue model (subscriptions + transaction fees)

**Pivot Options:**
- Focus on subscriptions only (forget transaction fees)
- Add other revenue streams (marketplace, affiliates, ads)
- White-label licensing to schools (one-time fees)

---

**3. Technical Execution (MEDIUM-HIGH RISK)**

**Description:** Founder struggles to build, project stalls, bugs undermine trust

**Probability:** Medium-High (40-50% for solo non-technical founder)

**Impact:** High (project fails or severely delayed)

**Mitigation:**
- 2-week prototype test (validate can build before committing)
- Phase gates (decision points to continue or pivot)
- Claude Code assistance (AI pair programming)
- Could pivot to partner/hire if prototype succeeds
- Well-documented (handoff-ready if needed)
- Start simple (MVP, not feature-complete)

**Validation:**
- End of Week 2: Working prototype or clear evidence can't build alone
- If can't build: Find technical co-founder or hire contractor

---

**4. Competition Response (LOW RISK)**

**Description:** Facebook improves, Eventbrite adds dance features, new entrant

**Probability:** Low (10-20%)

**Impact:** Medium (slows growth, but doesn't kill)

**Mitigation:**
- First-mover advantage in niche
- Network effects create moat (hard to displace once community there)
- Dance-specific hard to replicate generically
- Close community relationships (trust, not just features)
- Rapid iteration based on feedback

**Response:**
- Double down on community engagement
- Add unique features competitors can't easily copy
- Leverage existing users to defend (they won't switch easily)

---

**5. Market Size Too Small (LOW RISK — revised)**

**Description:** Addressable market insufficient to sustain business

**Probability:** Low (revised down from Medium — see below)

**Impact:** Medium (limits growth, but could still be profitable at small scale)

**Revised Assessment:** Original estimate of 9,000 UK dancers was Latin-only and understated. Revised tiered market (Latin ~18,000 + adjacent partner dance ~40,000) gives a realistic addressable market of 55,000–60,000 UK regular class attendees before any non-dance expansion. Scene-based growth model and flywheel dynamics further reduce this risk — the platform does not need large absolute numbers to be profitable, it needs critical mass within individual scenes.

**Mitigation:**
- Two-axis expansion model (geographic + dance style) provides multiple growth paths
- Phase 3 expansion to non-dance activity communities (yoga, martial arts, running clubs) is a longer-term option — same platform engine, configurable activity types
- Lifestyle business model doesn't need huge scale to succeed

---

**6. Economic Downturn (MEDIUM RISK)**

**Description:** Recession reduces dance class spending

**Probability:** Medium (30-40% over 5 years)

**Impact:** Medium (slows growth, increases churn)

**Mitigation:**
- Dance classes affordable (£8 vs gym £40/month)
- Social + exercise combo resilient (people need community)
- Platform doesn't depend on spending growth (free tier keeps users engaged)
- Subscription model more predictable than pure transaction
- Freemium model (users can downgrade, not churn completely)

**Response:**
- Emphasize value (save money with packages)
- Community support (emotional value during tough times)
- Flexible pricing (discounts, payment plans)

**Probability:** High (50-60% for solo founder with ADHD over 12 months)

**Impact:** High (project stalls or fails)

- Breaks and recovery time (avoid sprinting into burnout)
